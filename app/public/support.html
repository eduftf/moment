About Moment

Moment is a Zoom App that automatically captures screenshots during meetings. It works alongside a lightweight companion tool running on your computer. Screenshots can be triggered by a thumbs-up reaction, when participant count hits a new peak, or with a manual Capture Now button. Every screenshot is neatly organized into folders by meeting topic so you can revisit key moments later.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I install the companion tool?

Download the companion from the GitHub Releases page — pick the right build for your system (macOS Apple Silicon, macOS Intel, or Windows). Run it once and it will auto-start in the background on future logins.

Why does it say "Companion: Not running"?

This means the companion tool is not currently active on your machine. Moment communicates with the companion over a local connection. Make sure the companion is running — it auto-starts after the first launch, but you can also start it manually by double-clicking the downloaded file.

Where are screenshots saved?

Each meeting gets a full archive in ~/Moment/{meeting-topic}-{date}/. Inside you'll find an archive.html viewer with participant data, a timeline of events, and a screenshot gallery. Open it in any browser.

Can I change the save folder?

Yes. Open the Settings panel inside the Moment app during a meeting and you can configure a custom save location.

What triggers a screenshot?

There are three triggers: any emoji reaction during the meeting (you can choose which reactions to enable), a new participant peak (when the number of participants reaches a new high), or the manual Capture Now button inside the app.
