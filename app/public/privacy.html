Privacy Policy

Moment — a Zoom App by gtools.space (Mykhailo Gorkusha)
Effective date: February 26, 2026

Overview

Moment is a Zoom App that captures screenshots during meetings, triggered by emoji reactions, participant count peaks, or manual clicks. This policy explains what data Moment accesses, how it is used, and where it is stored.

Moment stores all data locally on your computer. No screenshots, meeting data, or personal information is sent to external servers.

What Data We Access

When you use Moment during a Zoom meeting, the app accesses the following information through the Zoom Apps SDK:

  • Meeting topic
  • Participant names and participant count
  • Emoji reactions
  • Timestamps of captured screenshots

Moment uses the zoomapp:inmeeting OAuth scope to read meeting context, detect reactions, and observe participant changes.

How We Use Your Data

The data accessed by Moment is used solely to:

  • Detect trigger events (reactions, participant peaks) for screenshot capture
  • Organize and label saved screenshots with relevant meeting context

We do not use your data for analytics, advertising, profiling, or any purpose other than the core functionality described above.

Data Storage

All screenshots and associated metadata are saved locally on your computer in the ~/Moment/ folder via a local companion CLI tool. Nothing is uploaded to cloud services or external servers operated by us or any third party.

Because your data never leaves your machine, you have full control over it. You can view, move, or delete your files at any time.

Third Parties

Moment does not share, sell, or transmit any data to third parties. The app communicates only with the Zoom client through the Zoom Apps SDK. No external APIs, analytics services, or advertising networks are used.

Cookies and Tracking

Moment does not use cookies, local storage tokens, analytics scripts, or any form of tracking technology.

Security

Since all data remains on your local machine, security depends on your own device protections (disk encryption, OS-level access controls, etc.). We recommend keeping your operating system and Zoom client up to date.

Children's Privacy

Moment is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected on this page with an updated effective date. Continued use of Moment after changes constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
